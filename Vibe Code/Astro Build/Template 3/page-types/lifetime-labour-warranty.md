# Lifetime Labour Warranty Page — Writing Guidelines

This doc covers the `/about/lifetime-labour-warranty/` page on every site in the network. This is the most legally and factually sensitive page on the site — what's covered, what's not, and how to claim must match what the business actually delivers.

Cross-references: `brand-voice-guide.md`, `seo-foundations.md`, `content-quality-rules.md`, `page-types/about.md` (parent).

## 1. Job-to-be-done

Document the terms of the Lifetime Labour Warranty so customers understand:
1. What is covered (workmanship faults)
2. What is not covered (misuse, third-party damage, wear and tear, product/part failure)
3. How to make a warranty claim
4. Why the warranty exists (trust signal)

This is **separate from** the Service Guarantee (`/about/service-guarantee/`). The Warranty is about what happens *after* the job if something fails due to our workmanship. The Service Guarantee is about how the job is performed in the first place.

## 2. Search intent

Trust-driven, conversion support. Customers research warranty terms before booking expensive jobs (hot water installs, pipe relining, gas fitting). Also a key page for AI search engines summarising the brand — clear warranty terms in plain text are high-signal for E-E-A-T.

## 3. Target queries

**Primary:**
- [site name] warranty
- [site name] labour warranty
- lifetime labour warranty plumber [primary suburb]
- plumber warranty [primary suburb]

**Secondary:**

## 5. Section structure (in order)

### 5.1 H1

**Pattern:** `Lifetime Labour Warranty` or `Our Lifetime Labour Warranty Explained`

Single phrase H1, no separators. Title tag may keep pipes for SERP readability.
**Variant:** `Our Lifetime Labour Warranty Explained`

### 5.2 H1 intro (35 words exactly)

Must include:
- Site name from `site.siteName`
- Primary suburb (1 mention)
- The core promise from `guarantees.warranty.description`
- Lic. #333997 reference

**Example:** `Every plumbing job Plumber Happy Valley completes across Happy Valley is backed by our Lifetime Labour Warranty. If a fault occurs due to our workmanship, we return and fix it at no cost. Lic. #333997.`

### 5.3 Why We Offer It (H2 + 60-80 word paragraph)

H2 heading: 12-15 words
**Pattern:** `Why We Back Every Job for Life`

60-80 word paragraph explaining the reason behind the warranty. Draws from `brand-voice-guide.md` Section 2 — the warranty exists because the business takes pride in workmanship and stakes its reputation on every job.

**Source content from the customer's existing site (verified):**
- Trust is earned, not assumed
- The warranty reflects pride in workmanship
- Built on long-term relationships with Adelaide homeowners

**Tone:** plainspoken, confident. Not a sales pitch.

### 5.4 What's Covered (H2 + 4-5 expanded dot points)

H2 heading: 12-15 words
**Pattern:** `What the Warranty Covers`

4-5 expanded dot points (bold heading + 20-30w explanation each). These are the verified terms from the customer's existing site — do not add or remove items without confirming with the business.

**Verified covered items:**

```
**Labour by a Plumber Happy Valley technician**
[20-30w — all workmanship performed by our licensed plumbers across the four primary services and their sub-services.]

**Plumbing, drain, and gas workmanship**
[20-30w — all plumbing, drain relining, gas fitting, and hot water installation work performed by our team.]

**Labour-related repairs caused by our installation**
[20-30w — no charge for return visits and labour costs to fix workmanship faults. If we did it wrong, we fix it for free.]

**No fine print, no runaround**
[20-30w — we assess the concern promptly and book a technician if needed. No unnecessary delays, no hidden fees on warranty claims.]
```

### 5.5 What's Not Covered (H2 + 3-4 expanded dot points)

H2 heading: 12-15 words
**Pattern:** `What the Warranty Does Not Cover`

3-4 expanded dot points covering the exclusions. These are the verified exclusions from the customer's existing site.

**Verified exclusion items:**

```
**Misuse, third-party damage, or lack of maintenance**
[20-30w — damage caused after the job by misuse, third parties, or failure to maintain the system is not covered.]

**Natural wear and tear**
[20-30w — fixtures, pipes, and systems naturally degrade over time. Wear and tear is not a workmanship fault and is not covered.]

**Product or part failure**
[20-30w — manufacturer faults are covered by the product's own warranty, not ours. We will help you claim against the manufacturer.]

**Work carried out by others after our job**
[20-30w — if another plumber, tradie, or DIYer has altered the system after we left, the warranty no longer applies to that area.]
```

### 5.6 How to Make a Claim (H2 + 4-5 numbered steps)

H2 heading: 12-15 words
**Pattern:** `How to Make a Warranty Claim`

Numbered list (1-5), each step 15-25 words. Practical, plain-spoken.

```
1. Call us on [site.trackingPhone] or use our website contact form to report the issue.

2. Briefly describe the issue, the original job, and when the work was completed.

3. We will assess the concern promptly and book a technician if a site visit is needed.

4. The technician will diagnose the cause and confirm whether it is a workmanship fault.

5. If it is our workmanship, we return and fix it at no cost to you.
```

### 5.7 Related Guarantees (H2 + 2 box hub)

H2 heading: 12-15 words
**Pattern:** `Our Other Guarantees`

**Layout:** 2-box grid.

1. **Service Guarantee** → `/about/service-guarantee/`
   - 30-word block: what we promise on how the job is performed.

2. **Financing Options** → `/about/financing/`
   - 30-word block: spread the cost of larger jobs with Humm.

### 5.8 Service Areas (H2 + 30-word paragraph + bullet list)

Same format as `about.md` Section 5.7.

### 5.9 Final CTA (H2 + 30-word paragraph)

H2 heading: 12-15 words
**Pattern:** `Book Your [Primary Suburb] Plumber, Backed for Life`

Paragraph: 30 words. Must include:
- Phone from `site.trackingPhone`
- Warranty reference
- One offer from `offers.newCustomerDiscount` or `offers.pensionerDiscount`

## 10. Anti-patterns (NEVER on the warranty page)

1. **Invent warranty terms.** Every covered or not-covered item must match the verified terms from the business. Adding or removing items without confirmation is a legal risk.
2. **Promise the warranty covers products or parts.** It does not. The warranty is labour only. Manufacturer warranties cover products.
3. **Promise free return visits for non-workmanship issues.** Wear and tear, misuse, third-party damage, and product failure are all not covered.
4. **Hedge the warranty.** Phrases like "we usually return" or "in most cases" weaken the promise. The warranty is unambiguous: if it is our workmanship, we fix it at no cost.
5. **Use weasel language about claim assessment.** "We may assess at our discretion" is hostile. The verified language is "we will assess promptly" — confident and clear.
6. **Embellish what's covered.** Adding "and parts" or "and product failure" to the covered list creates false expectations and legal risk.
7. **Cross-link to other sites in the network.** Per `seo-foundations.md` Part C4.
8. **Use em dashes.** Per `brand-voice-guide.md`.
9. **Pad to hit 700+ words.** Clarity beats length on legal/trust pages.
10. **Mention air conditioning, HVAC, electrical, or any non-plumbing service.** Per `content-quality-rules.md` Part C3.
11. **Reference a specific dollar value or claim limit unless verified in site-config.** No "$10,000 cap" or "up to 12 months" — the warranty is lifetime on labour, no cap on labour cost.
12. **Conflate with Service Guarantee.** These are two separate pages, two separate concepts.
13. **Pipes (`|`) or other separators in H1 or H2 headings.** Single phrases on-page. Title tag meta only.
14. **Straight dot-point sections with no intro.** Every covered/not-covered list needs a 1-2 sentence lead before the bullets.
