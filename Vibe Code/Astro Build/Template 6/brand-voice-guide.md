## 1. The Micro-Site Network

### What This Network Is

This is a network of 100 suburb-specific plumbing websites across Adelaide and surrounding regions. Each site uses a localised domain (for example plumberhappyvalley.com.au, plumbernorwood.com.au, plumberprospect.com.au) and is positioned as a local plumber serving that specific suburb.

### Why The Network Exists

Adelaide homeowners search for plumbers locally. They want a tradie who knows their suburb, understands the common issues in the area, and feels close to home rather than a generic, faceless company. Each micro-site is designed to feel like the local plumber for that suburb, while sharing one set of underlying values, standards, and operations behind the scenes.

### How To Think About Each Site

Treat every micro-site as if it were the local plumbing business for that one suburb. The brand identity is hyper-local. The suburb name belongs in the brand wherever the site name appears, in service descriptions, and throughout the messaging. The same operator, the same licensed team, and the same standards sit behind every site, but each site speaks to its own community.

**Local  .  Direct  .  Trustworthy**

### How The Brand Sounds

The brand sounds like a confident, experienced local plumber having a straight conversation with a homeowner. Not a salesperson. Not a corporate voice. Not a casual mate. Knowledgeable, warm, and direct. The kind of person who gives a real answer and gets on with the job.

- Corporate, distant, or full of business jargon
- Overly casual, slangy, or unprofessional
- Salesy, pushy, or hype-driven
- Vague, hedging, or full of weasel words
- Generic, interchangeable with any other plumbing site

## 7. Voice In Practice

This section covers how the brand actually writes. Follow these rules so 100 sites read like one consistent business across every page.

### Voice Mechanics

| Rule | Standard |
|---|---|
| **Spelling** | Australian English. Always. (organise, colour, recognise, licence as a noun / license as a verb, metres, kilometres, neighbour) |
| **Person** | "We" and "us" inside body copy, paired with "Plumber [Suburb]" as the named brand in titles, intros, and structured data. Never refer to the brand as "they" or in third person inside its own copy. |
| **Customer reference** | Always "you" and "your home". Never "client", "customer", "individual" inside conversational copy. The word "homeowner" is fine in technical or SEO copy, not in headlines or direct address. |
| **Team reference** | "Our team", "our local plumbers", "our licensed plumbers". Avoid "technicians", "operators", "professionals", "experts" as standalone nouns. |
| **Contractions** | Yes. We're, we'll, don't, won't, can't, you're, it's. Contractions make the voice human. Avoid them only in headlines where rhythm calls for the full form. |
| **Sentence length** | Short and medium. Aim for an average of 12 to 18 words per sentence. One-word and two-word sentences are fine for emphasis. Long compound sentences are not. |
| **Reading level** | Grade 7 to 8. A 14-year-old should be able to read it without stumbling. |
| **Punctuation** | No em dashes ever. Use commas, full stops, or a new sentence instead. Oxford commas optional, prefer dropping them. Exclamation marks used sparingly, never more than one in any block of copy. Semicolons avoided. |
| **Numbers** | Numerals for anything 10 and above and for prices, times, and stats ($50, 24/7, 10%, 500+). Spell out one to nine in body copy. |
| **Capitalisation** | Sentence case for headlines and body. Title Case is reserved for proper nouns and structured page headers only. Trust signals can be capitalised when used as named features (Lifetime Labour Warranty, Fixed Upfront Pricing). |

### Vocabulary: Use These Words

These words pull their weight. They reinforce the brand promise and sound like the voice.

**Action and reliability:** local, properly, on time, show up, fixed, upfront, sorted, done right, the first time, licensed, insured, warranted, real, honest, straight

**Service language:** plumbing, plumber, fix, clear, replace, install, repair, service, emergency, blocked, burst, leaking

**Trust and credibility:** family-owned, locally run, decade of experience, hundreds of 5-star reviews, Lifetime Labour Warranty, Fixed Upfront Pricing

**Customer-side:** your home, your family, your suburb, your time, your peace of mind

### Vocabulary: Avoid These Words

Banned. They sound like every other plumbing website.

**Empty superlatives:** best, leading, top-rated, world-class, premier, premium, elite, market-leading, industry-leading

**Corporate filler:** solutions, offerings, deliverables, value-add, leverage, synergy, holistic, end-to-end, seamless, streamlined

**Vague hedging:** aim to, try to, strive to, endeavour to, committed to providing, dedicated to ensuring

**Salesy clichés:** unbeatable, unmatched, second to none, no job too big or too small, your one-stop shop, satisfaction guaranteed (the warranty is the guarantee, do not water it down)

**Bad customer references:** client, individuals, valued customer, dear customer

### Australian Tone Calibration

The voice is Australian, not American and not overly British. It uses some Australian terms naturally without leaning into broad Strine.

**Use naturally:** tradie, mate (in casual contexts only, never in headlines or service pages), sorted, dodgy (when describing the bad-tradie problem the brand solves), bloke (sparingly, used for warmth not jokes)

**Avoid:** g'day, fair dinkum, no worries cobber, any forced Aussie-isms. The brand is Australian by default, it does not perform being Australian.

### How To Talk About The Suburb

The local angle is the brand's biggest differentiator across 100 sites. Done well it feels native. Done poorly it reads as keyword-stuffing.

**Do:**
- Name the suburb in the opening sentence of every page
- Reference real local context where relevant (older homes in [Suburb] often have, common in [Suburb] is, [Suburb] homes typically)
- Mention neighbouring suburbs and landmarks naturally to reinforce locality
- Talk about the suburb as if writing for residents who already live there

**Do not:**
- Repeat the suburb name more than three or four times per 300 words
- Force the suburb into headlines where it does not fit naturally
- Invent local context that is not real (do not claim "the historic streets of Salisbury" if Salisbury is not historic)
- Write the same suburb-specific paragraph across multiple sites with only the suburb name swapped out

## 8. Channel Calibration

The voice stays the same across channels. The format and length flex.

### Homepage Headline
Short, direct, suburb-named. Lead with locality and reliability. Eight to twelve words maximum.

*Example: Your local Happy Valley plumber. On time. Upfront pricing. Done properly.*

### Service Page Paragraph
Conversational, problem-led, ends in trust signals. Two to four short sentences per paragraph.

*Example: A blocked drain in Happy Valley is rarely just a clog. Older homes around here often have tree root intrusion, and the longer it sits, the worse it gets. We use CCTV cameras to find the cause, then clear it with high-pressure hydrojetting. Fixed price before we start. Backed by our Lifetime Labour Warranty.*

### Google Business Post
Helpful, local, short. One useful idea per post. 40 to 80 words. No salesy openers.

*Example: A dripping tap wastes around 20,000 litres a year. If you've got one going in Happy Valley, give us a call. Fixed upfront price, sorted same day in most cases.*

### Review Reply
Warm, brief, no template feel. Thank, reference the job, sign off as the team.

*Example: Thanks Sarah, really appreciate it. Glad we could sort the hot water out before the weekend. Any time you need us, you know where we are. The Plumber Happy Valley team.*

### SMS Confirmation
Plain text, no exclamation marks, sounds like a person.

*Example: Hi Sarah, this is Plumber Happy Valley. Booked in for tomorrow between 9 and 11am. Any issues just text back.*

### Footer / About Snippet
Tight, factual, every trust signal in one paragraph. Includes the transparency line (see Section 9 below).

*Example: Plumber Happy Valley is a local, family-owned plumbing business serving Happy Valley and the surrounding suburbs. Lic. #333997. ABN 52 668 135 886. Lifetime Labour Warranty. Fixed upfront pricing. 24/7 emergency response. $50 off your first service.*

## 9. Network Transparency Rule

The 100 micro-sites are not 100 separate businesses. They are one licensed Adelaide plumbing operation presenting itself locally to each suburb it services. This is honest, legal, and the only defensible way to run a network of this scale.

Every site must disclose the shared ABN and licence in the footer. Visible, not hidden. This is what protects the entire network from being classified as fake-local doorway pages.

### The transparency line (appears in the footer of every site)

> *Lic. #333997. ABN 52 668 135 886.*

### What this gets right

- **Same ABN and same licence** on every site, honestly disclosed in the footer
- **The local positioning is honest**: "servicing [Suburb]" rather than claiming a physical premises in the suburb
- **No fake locals**: the network never pretends to be 100 separate businesses
