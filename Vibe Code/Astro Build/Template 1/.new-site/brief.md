# Brand Brief

## Positioning
- **Business:** Network of 100 suburb-specific plumbing micro-sites across greater Adelaide, all backed by one licensed family-owned operation.
- **Ideal Customer:** Adelaide homeowner, 35–65, has been let down by a tradie before. Not shopping on price — shopping on trust, locality, and certainty they'll actually show up.
- **Primary CTA:** Call now (phone number, emergency-first)
- **Secondary CTA:** Get a quote / Book online
- **Differentiator:** Lifetime Labour Warranty on every job. Fixed upfront pricing. 24/7 including weekends and public holidays. Within-the-hour response. $50 off first service. 500+ 5-star reviews from Adelaide homeowners. Local, family-owned — not a franchise, not a national chain.
- **Positioning Statement:** Plumber [Suburb] is the local, family-owned plumbing business for [Suburb] homes. Fixed upfront pricing. A Lifetime Labour Warranty. 24/7 emergency response. One licensed Adelaide team that shows up on time, does the job properly, and treats your home with respect.

## Brand Voice & Tone
- **Personality:** Local, Direct, Trustworthy
- **Tone:** Confident, experienced, straight-talking. Like a licensed tradie having a real conversation with a homeowner — not a salesperson, not a corporate voice.
- **We are:** Local, reliable, honest, professional, human, family-owned, plain-spoken
- **We are NOT:** Corporate, distant, salesy, pushy, vague, generic, slangy, overly casual

## Visual Identity

### Color Constraints
None — designer's choice per concept.

### Font Constraints
None — designer's choice per concept.

> Final palette and fonts are selected per concept direction during brainstorm.

### Visual Style
- **Keywords:** Local, residential, trustworthy, direct, Australian
- **Inspiration patterns:** Reference site (jimmysparkselectrical.com.au) — trust-heavy, phone-first, modular, photography-forward, family business warmth, amber accent
- **Do:** Hyper-local suburb focus. Trust signals prominent and early. Phone number always visible. Emergency response messaging above the fold.
- **Don't:** Corporate blue palettes. Salesy superlatives. Generic tradie aesthetic. Empty template sections.
