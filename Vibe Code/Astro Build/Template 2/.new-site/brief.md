# Brand Brief

## Positioning
- **Business:** A network of 100 suburb-specific plumbing websites across Adelaide and surrounding regions, each site targeting one suburb (e.g. plumberhappyvalley.com.au).
- **Ideal Customer:** Adelaide homeowners aged 35–65 who have been let down by tradies before — not looking for the cheapest option, looking for someone they can trust in their local area who will actually show up and do what they say.
- **Primary CTA:** Call now (phone number)
- **Differentiator:** Local family-owned business, Lifetime Labour Warranty on every job, Fixed Upfront Pricing (no hourly, no bill shock), Within-the-Hour Response for emergencies, $50 off first service.
- **Positioning Statement:** Plumber [Suburb] is the local, family-owned plumbing business serving [Suburb] homes with fixed upfront pricing, a Lifetime Labour Warranty, and 24/7 emergency response. Behind every job is a licensed Adelaide team with a decade of experience and a simple promise: show up on time, do the job properly, and treat the home with respect.

## Brand Voice & Tone
- **Personality:** Local, Direct, Trustworthy, Professional, Human
- **Tone:** Confident, experienced local plumber having a straight conversation with a homeowner — not a salesperson, not corporate, not overly casual. Knowledgeable, warm, and direct.
- **Brand Promise:** "Show up on time. Do the job properly. Treat the home with respect."
- **We are:** Local, family-owned, reliable, honest, licensed, upfront about pricing, backed by real warranties
- **We are NOT:** Corporate, distant, salesy, vague, generic, interchangeable with any other plumbing site
- **Spelling:** Australian English always (organise, colour, licence as noun/license as verb)

### Visual Style
- **Keywords:** Professional, trustworthy, local, direct, human
- **Inspiration:** jimmysparkselectrical.com.au — professional trades site with dark header/footer, orange CTA accent, trust badge strips, service grids, social proof, and clear information hierarchy
- **Do:** Trust signals prominently displayed, clear phone CTA in header, strong hero with suburb-specific headline, services grid, comparison table, FAQ section, service area map/list
- **Don't:** Corporate jargon, vague claims, lorem ipsum, overcrowded layouts without hierarchy
